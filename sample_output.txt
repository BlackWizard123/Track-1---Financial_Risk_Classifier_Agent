YOU :
I am planning to quit my corporate job in six months to start a freelance consulting business. I have ₹10 Lakhs in savings, monthly expenses of ₹60,000, and a home loan EMI of ₹25,000. If my business takes four months to earn its first rupee, what is my 'ruin probability' and how many months of buffer do I actually have?

AI RESPONSE :
📊 Risk Level: High

🧠 Summary:
You are facing a high-risk financial situation due to a planned career transition from stable employment to an unproven freelance business with a significant initial income gap, combined with high fixed expenses and a major liability (home loan). While you have some savings, the buffer is not extensive enough given the unknowns.

⚠️ Key Reason:
Transitioning from a stable corporate job to a freelance consulting business introduces significant income instability. Your monthly expenses of ₹85,000 (₹60,000 living expenses + ₹25,000 home loan EMI) are substantial. With a planned 4-month period of zero income after quitting, ₹3.4 Lakhs of your ₹10 Lakhs savings will be depleted, leaving you with ₹6.6 Lakhs. This translates to an actual buffer of approximately 7.76 months (₹6.6 Lakhs / ₹85,000 per month) after the initial no-income period. The 'ruin probability' is high if your business does not generate sufficient income to cover expenses within this remaining buffer, or if it takes longer than 4 months to earn its first rupee.

✅ Improvements:
• Significantly increase your savings buffer (aim for 18-24 months of expenses) before quitting your corporate job.
• Begin building your freelance consulting business part-time while still employed to secure initial clients and income, mitigating the 4-month income gap.
• Develop a detailed and conservative business plan with clear income projections, client acquisition strategies, and a timeline for profitability.
• Actively reduce current non-essential monthly expenses (the ₹60,000 portion) to extend your financial runway and lower your burn rate.
• Explore potential contingency plans, such as short-term contract work, if the freelance business takes longer than expected to become self-sustaining.

📈 Stability Plan:
• Aggressively save every possible rupee over the next six months to boost your emergency fund.
• Dedicate time during your current employment to setting up your freelance business, including creating a portfolio, identifying target clients, and networking.
• Create a strict budget for your post-quitting period, prioritizing essential expenses and eliminating discretionary spending.
• Establish clear financial milestones for your business (e.g., minimum monthly income targets) and a defined timeline for re-evaluating your strategy if these are not met.
• Maintain open communication with your lender regarding your home loan, exploring options like interest-only payments or temporary deferment if severe cash flow issues arise, though this should be a last resort.
